# Premium 003/004 Longer Hybrid Validation Design

## Goal

Create a longer premium validation sample that tests the real upper bound of this pipeline instead of flattering it with a very short cut.

The sample should still focus on the emotional peak around the current `shot-003` and `shot-004` material, but it should now answer a harder and more useful question:

Can this pipeline produce a 24-30 second vertical AI manhua clip that still feels intentional once the viewer has enough time to notice speech, clarity, continuity, rhythm, and acting problems?

The recommended target is a `26-28 second` sample, because that is long enough to expose problems honestly without exploding scope.

## Problem Statement

The recent premium sample improved still-image quality a lot, but the result still does not feel like characters are performing inside a scene.

The user's current diagnosis is correct:

- mouths do not move convincingly while speaking
- motion softens detail and makes the image feel slightly blurred
- acting feels stiff
- the whole result still reads too much like a moving poster with voice pasted on top

There are also three structural issues behind those symptoms:

1. the current motion stage is good at subtle animated illustration, not at speech performance
2. the current very short sample hides problems instead of exposing them
3. the local default voice path sounds cheap enough to drag down otherwise improved visuals

Because of this, the next iteration should not try to solve everything at once. It should use a longer and more honest sample while minimizing the number of shots that require visible lip-sync success.

## Design Principles

1. one premium visual source
   Cloud still generation continues to define the visual ceiling. Local tools extend that look instead of inventing a second one.

2. expose weakness honestly
   The sample should be long enough that clarity, rhythm, acting, and continuity issues become visible.

3. speech is rare and deliberate
   Visible mouth movement should be used only where the payoff is worth the risk.

4. voice identity stays unified
   Narration and inner voice should feel like the same female character voice with only light treatment differences.

5. longer does not mean wider
   This is still a narrow validation sample, not a production factory or a full episode remake.

6. each shot needs intent
   Every shot must have a clear dramatic task and a simple action goal, not just an image plus camera push.

## Scope

This design covers one revised validation sample:

- project id: `premium-003-004`
- target length: `24-30 seconds`
- recommended length: `26-28 seconds`
- target structure: `5 shots`
- voice structure:
  - 1 narration segment
  - 1 inner-voice segment
  - 1 spoken dialogue segment
  - 2 silent bridge shots
- visual target: premium national-style manhua, semi-painted cinematic rendering
- emotional target: intimate oppressive danger, not generic romance and not simple hostility

This design includes:

- revised five-shot structure
- revised text strategy for narration, inner voice, and spoken line
- higher-quality external voice source requirement
- motion strategy matched to shot purpose
- review-friendly render outputs
- updated acceptance criteria

This design does not include:

- full-episode production
- large-scale automation
- generalized dual-provider orchestration
- full lip-sync solving across many speaking shots
- cost optimization

## Quality Target

The desired look remains:

- premium national-style manhua
- semi-painted cinematic rendering
- mature facial structure
- mobile-readable silhouette
- strong moonlit atmosphere
- controlled negative space

The revised quality bar is stricter than before:

- a still must work as a screenshot
- the motion version must not noticeably cheapen the still
- the longer cut must still hold together once the viewer has time to notice details
- the audio must no longer sound like a default local system voice

The sample should explicitly avoid:

- round or childlike faces
- oversized anime eyes
- soft idol styling
- generic romance-poster composition
- motion that is only a slow zoom
- obvious pasted-on speech

## Character And Relationship Target

### Su Wan

She should read as:

- mature
- sharp
- intelligent
- dangerous in a controlled way

Her screen presence should survive both silent reaction shots and inner-voice shots without needing exaggerated facial animation.

### Regent Prince

He should read as:

- cold
- hard
- structurally handsome rather than soft
- capable of changing the scene temperature through proximity alone

### Pair Dynamic

Together they should read as:

- unequal in power
- dangerous in proximity
- emotionally charged
- visually compatible in maturity and status

The scene should feel like pressure that borders on intimacy, not like sweet romance and not like ordinary palace hostility.

## Storytelling Strategy

This sample should use the approved `B` hybrid structure:

- one compressed narration beat
- one inner-voice beat
- one short spoken line
- two silent visual bridge beats

This is the key tradeoff:

- the sample becomes long enough to expose real problems
- but only one shot is asked to carry visible spoken performance

## Text Strategy

The writing must be compressed and performance-aware.

### Narration

- only carry essential scene setup
- avoid over-explaining plot
- sound like a concise dramatic cue, not a paragraph

### Inner Voice

- feel like an immediate private judgment
- stay close in tone and identity to the female narration voice
- avoid cartoonish "inner monologue" styling

### Spoken Dialogue

- only one spoken line in the whole sample
- keep it short, hard, and pause-friendly
- the line should be worth risking a speaking close-up

## Five-Shot Structure

### Shot 1: Establishing Pressure

Purpose:

- establish the palace corridor atmosphere
- place Su Wan inside the space before facial scrutiny becomes intense
- deliver compressed narration

Duration:

- `4-5 seconds`

Voice type:

- `narration`

Action goal:

- start still, then introduce a small posture or eye-line change

Visual direction:

- wider or medium framing
- visible architecture, moonlight, pillar shadow, and clear staging
- do not open with an aggressive face close-up

Motion direction:

- subtle spatial drift
- slight atmospheric movement
- no simple full-shot slow zoom as the only change

### Shot 2: Silent Reaction Close-Up

Purpose:

- let Su Wan feel present without forcing mouth performance
- shift from scene setup into character tension

Duration:

- `4-5 seconds`

Voice type:

- `silent`

Action goal:

- react through breath, gaze, shoulder, or neck tension

Visual direction:

- closer framing than Shot 1
- face readable, but not posed like a poster
- expression should tighten rather than decorate

Motion direction:

- tiny subject movement plus light camera pressure
- no theatrical gesture

### Shot 3: Inner-Voice Beat

Purpose:

- bring in Su Wan's subjectivity
- deepen tension while avoiding a lip-sync-heavy shot

Duration:

- `4-5 seconds`

Voice type:

- `inner_voice`

Action goal:

- slight head, eye, or body adjustment that supports the thought beat

Visual direction:

- three-quarter view, partial obstruction, or composition that reduces mouth scrutiny
- close enough to feel intimate, but not straight-on mouth display

Motion direction:

- restrained face-area motion
- support mood rather than obvious speech

### Shot 4: Silent Pressure Transition

Purpose:

- build relationship tension between them
- create the bridge into the hero speaking shot

Duration:

- `5-6 seconds`

Voice type:

- `silent`

Action goal:

- one clear relationship change, such as him closing distance or her refusing to retreat

Visual direction:

- two-shot, over-shoulder, or charged detail framing
- use spatial compression, hand detail, sleeve movement, or eye-line pressure
- this must not be a decorative filler shot

Motion direction:

- layered movement across both subject and framing
- pressure should build through distance change, not just lens drift

### Shot 5: Spoken Hero Shot

Purpose:

- test the current upper bound of visible spoken performance
- end on the strongest pressure beat

Duration:

- `5-7 seconds`, including a short post-line hold

Voice type:

- `spoken`

Action goal:

- deliver one short line, then let the shot breathe briefly after it lands

Visual direction:

- oppressive close-up or dominant near-close framing
- prefer three-quarter or pressure-angle composition over flat front-facing mouth display
- the face and distance should carry the danger before subtitles do

Motion direction:

- the strongest acting shot in the sample
- still restrained, but clearly more performance-oriented than the other four shots

## Audio Design

The current local default voice path should be retired for this sample.

Use a better external voice source, such as curated downloadable voice packs or a higher-quality TTS path.

This iteration should support only two core voices:

1. female voice
   - handles narration and inner voice
   - same identity, same timbre family
   - only light treatment difference between the two modes

2. male voice
   - handles the single spoken line
   - should sound premium enough to support a hero close-up

The sample should not introduce extra voice roles or a complex voice system yet.

## Motion Design Rules

To avoid the "moving poster" feel, every shot must follow all of these rules:

1. no shot survives only by a slow push-in
2. each shot needs both:
   - a subject-side change
   - a framing or environmental change
3. the spoken shot should avoid a flat frontal mouth showcase
4. shot transitions should advance relationship pressure, not just swap camera angles

## Pipeline Adjustments Required

This design implies four necessary pipeline changes.

### 1. Script And Shot Schema

Each shot should explicitly carry:

- voice type: `silent`, `narration`, `inner_voice`, or `spoken`
- action goal
- motion intent

This prevents the pipeline from treating all shots as the same "image plus line" unit.

### 2. Audio Source Layer

The current default local voice should no longer be the only built-in path.

The sample needs a switchable higher-quality voice source that can supply:

- one female identity for narration plus inner voice
- one male identity for the spoken line

### 3. Motion Template Layer

Motion behavior should be chosen by shot purpose, not reused as one generic template across all shots.

At minimum, the pipeline should distinguish:

- establishing atmosphere
- silent reaction
- inner-voice intimacy
- silent relationship pressure
- spoken hero performance

### 4. Review Outputs

The final output should support diagnosis, not just delivery.

Besides the final render, keep:

- per-shot video clips
- per-shot audio files
- a shot review manifest or checklist

## Selection Rules

Shot stills and motion clips should be judged more strictly than before.

A shot is promoted only if:

- the still works on its own
- the motion version does not noticeably cheapen the still
- the shot still feels intentional inside a longer sequence
- the voice and image feel like they belong to the same scene

A shot is rejected if:

- the face collapses into a generic template
- motion introduces obvious blur or facial softness
- the voice instantly lowers perceived quality
- the shot only works when isolated and falls apart in sequence

## Stop-Loss Rules

1. voice stop-loss
   If the replacement voice path still sounds distracting or cheap, pause and replace the voice source before polishing visuals further.

2. spoken-shot stop-loss
   If the hero speaking shot repeatedly fails, first reduce mouth visibility or shorten the line before drawing more video lottery tickets.

3. sequence stop-loss
   If the sample only feels good when cut back below the target length, treat that as a failed validation rather than quietly shortening it.

## Deliverables

This revised validation should produce:

1. updated `premium-003-004` project assets
2. revised `shots.json` with five shots
3. still candidates and one selected still per final shot
4. higher-quality voice assets or external audio outputs
5. per-shot video clips

## Acceptance Criteria

The design is successful only if all of the following are true:

1. visual quality
   The selected stills remain premium in both screenshot and motion form.

2. audio quality
   The voices no longer sound like default local system speech.

3. continuity
   The five shots feel like one scene rather than five unrelated animated cards.

4. performance hierarchy
   The silent, narrated, inner-voice, and spoken shots each do a distinct job, and the single spoken shot feels like the peak rather than an awkward outlier.

5. honest exposure
   The longer cut reveals real problems instead of hiding them through brevity.

6. user threshold
   The user feels this result is a more truthful and more useful quality-ceiling sample than the previous shorter render.

## Future Boundary

That is a valid future direction, but it is intentionally out of scope for this iteration.

This iteration exists to prove a simpler point first:

Can a longer hybrid sample feel meaningfully better without requiring many visible lip-sync shots?
